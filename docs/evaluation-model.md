# ThinkingOS Evaluation Model

ThinkingOS evaluates the quality of a user's reasoning, not the user's intelligence, character, or worth. Evaluation should be evidence-based, transparent, and calibrated to the information available.

## Dimensions

### Input Quality

Assesses whether the supplied facts, goals, evidence, assumptions, constraints, and definitions are relevant, credible, clear, and sufficient for the intended analysis.

### Logic Quality

Assesses whether conclusions follow from premises, causal claims are justified, alternatives are considered, and common reasoning errors or unsupported leaps are avoided.

### Completeness

Assesses whether material inputs, stakeholders, constraints, alternatives, risks, and unanswered questions have been considered. Completeness is relative to the validated goal, not the volume of analysis.

### Consistency

Assesses whether claims, assumptions, criteria, and recommendations are internally compatible and remain stable across the reasoning process. Legitimate changes should be explicitly explained.

### Actionability

Assesses whether the reasoning supports a clear decision, test, question, or next step with sufficient ownership, priority, and constraints to be useful.

## Scoring and confidence

Implementations may score each dimension using a documented scale. Scores must be accompanied by strengths, weaknesses, missing information, logical issues, and a recommendation. Confidence reflects the reliability of the evaluation, not the desirability of the conclusion, and must decrease when evidence is incomplete, ambiguous, or low quality.

Dimensions must remain distinct: for example, a complete argument can still be logically weak, and a logically sound argument can remain non-actionable because critical inputs are missing.
