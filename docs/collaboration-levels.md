# Collaboration Levels

The collaboration level must be proportional to consequence, reversibility, uncertainty, and contract impact. More actors are not inherently better.

## Level definitions

| Level | Composition | Use when | Required controls |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| L1 — Single | One actor executes one Skill | Routine, low-cost, reversible thinking | Schema validation, disclosed assumptions, normal human decision ownership |
| L2 — Adversarial | Analyst plus critic, optionally an evidence reviewer | Material cost, difficult reversal, disputed causality, or consequential uncertainty | Shared request identity, single arbiter, divergence diagnosis, one committed state successor |
| L3 — Full panel | Independent roles, arbitration record, controlled role rotation | Schema or Skill changes, safety, legal, high-cost, or cross-organization decisions | Synthetic conformance evidence, explicit human approval, full record and state chains, trusted release anchor |

## Escalation

Escalate when any of these conditions becomes material:

- the decision is difficult to reverse;
- failure has safety, legal, financial, or broad stakeholder consequences;
- evidence is weak or causal explanations remain contested;
- actors produce repeatable incompatible classifications;
- the proposed change modifies a stable ThinkingOS contract.

Do not escalate merely because another model is available. De-escalate when the disputed variable is resolved and the remaining work is routine.

## L1 protocol

One actor receives a pinned framework and Skill identity, validated inputs, serialized state, and output schema. The host validates the result and the human retains decision ownership. L1 is the default.

## L2 protocol

1. Create candidate runs from the same base state and case identity.
2. Assign different cognitive roles without permanently binding roles to providers.
3. Keep candidate outputs immutable and prevent direct state writes.
4. Diagnose differences before evaluating conclusions.
5. Produce one arbitration record and one committed successor state.

Role rotation is recommended when the same platform repeatedly occupies one role or when conclusions appear role-dependent.

## L3 protocol

L3 adds independent evidence review, controlled role rotation, complete audit records, explicit human approval, and release-grade conformance. A delegated agent or policy may prepare arbitration, but cannot satisfy the L3 human-approval requirement.

For a stable contract release, conformance must cover at least one structured-output provider adapter and the MCP adapter. Live interoperability uses synthetic or explicitly authorized non-sensitive inputs.

## Decision rule

Arbitration is based on evidence, contract conformance, Never Rules, rubric findings, uncertainty, and the validated goal. It is never a majority vote. A blocking failure cannot be averaged away by several positive outputs.

## Feedback

Classified divergence feeds the appropriate improvement path. Only a repeatable contract ambiguity becomes a contract regression test; other findings improve adapters, validation, provider configuration, or host controls.
