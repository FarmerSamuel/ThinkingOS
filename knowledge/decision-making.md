# Decision-Making Knowledge

## Purpose

This reference supplies reusable concepts for skills that frame or evaluate decisions. It does not choose an option or replace accountable human judgment.

## Decision frame

A useful decision frame includes:

- A validated objective and decision owner.
- Alternatives, including maintaining the current state when relevant.
- Criteria tied to the objective.
- Constraints defining feasibility.
- Evidence and uncertainty for material claims.
- Trade-offs, reversibility, timing, and cost of error.

## Evidence and uncertainty

Separate observed facts, forecasts, preferences, assumptions, and unknowns. Confidence should decrease when evidence is indirect, stale, selected after the conclusion, or unable to discriminate between alternatives.

## Heuristics

Heuristics can reduce effort when decisions repeat and error costs are bounded. A reusable heuristic declares:

1. The context in which it applies.
2. The signal or rule used.
3. Expected benefits and costs.
4. Known failure conditions.
5. A trigger for escalation to deeper analysis.

## Common reasoning risks

- Treating a preferred option as the problem definition.
- Evaluating only one alternative.
- Confusing sunk cost with future value.
- Using a precise score to hide weak evidence.
- Ignoring base rates, reversibility, or second-order effects.
- Allowing urgency or authority to substitute for criteria.
