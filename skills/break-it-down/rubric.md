# Break It Down Rubric

| Score | Meaning |
| ---: | --- |
| 0 | Missing, contradictory, or unusable. |
| 1 | Materially incomplete, mixed, or over-broad. |
| 2 | Directionally useful with important gaps or overlap. |
| 3 | Sufficiently complete, distinct, tractable, and goal-aligned. |
| 4 | Comprehensive, evidence-backed, dependency-aware, and robust to alternative decompositions. |

## Calibration anchors

- Coverage 4 requires evidence that material in-scope areas were checked, not merely a long list.
- Distinctness 4 requires explicit treatment of unavoidable cross-cutting concerns.
- Tractability 4 requires documented stopping criteria.
- Dependency 4 requires sequencing and coupling to be testable.
- Goal Alignment 4 requires every leaf to trace to the validated goal.

When evidence fits two levels, select the lower unless the higher level's defining condition is demonstrated.
