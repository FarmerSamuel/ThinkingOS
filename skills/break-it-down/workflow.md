# Break It Down Workflow

1. **Validate entry:** Confirm a validated problem and clear goal are available.
2. **Select basis:** Choose one coherent decomposition basis: process, structure, cause, responsibility, time, or system boundary.
3. **Generate components:** Divide the whole into candidate parts without mixing bases at the same level.
4. **Test coverage:** Check that the parts collectively represent the in-scope problem.
5. **Test overlap:** Remove or explain duplicated responsibility between parts.
6. **Refine units:** Continue until each leaf is understandable, evaluable, and actionable without hiding another broad problem.
7. **Map dependencies:** Record prerequisites, shared inputs, sequencing, and coupling.
8. **Evaluate:** Score coverage, distinctness, tractability, dependency integrity, and goal alignment.
9. **Output and transition:** Return the component tree, limitations, confidence, and a justified next skill.

Stop when the declared stopping criteria are met. More detail is not automatically better; over-decomposition that destroys meaning is a validation failure.
