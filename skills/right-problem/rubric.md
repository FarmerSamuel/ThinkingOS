# Right Problem Rubric

## Shared scale

| Score | Meaning |
| ---: | --- |
| 0 | Absent, unusable, or contradicted. |
| 1 | Present but materially unclear, unsupported, or misclassified. |
| 2 | Partially usable with important gaps or assumptions. |
| 3 | Clear and sufficiently supported for the current decision. |
| 4 | Precise, evidence-backed, bounded, and resilient to plausible alternatives. |

## Goal Score anchors

- **0:** No goal, or the “goal” is only a preselected solution.
- **2:** A desired outcome exists but lacks scope or success evidence.
- **4:** Outcome, stakeholder, scope, and success criteria are explicit and relevant.

## Obstacle Score anchors

- **0:** No obstacle, or it merely repeats the problem.
- **2:** A plausible obstacle is named but causality or evidence is weak.
- **4:** The obstacle is specific, evidence-backed, and tested against alternative explanations.

## Constraint Score anchors

- **0:** Material constraints are ignored or contradicted.
- **2:** Some constraints are listed but classification or relevance is uncertain.
- **4:** Material constraints are complete, relevant, and classified as fixed, negotiable, assumed, or unknown.

## Logic Score anchors

- **0:** Goal, obstacle, evidence, and conclusion contradict one another.
- **2:** The frame is plausible but contains an unsupported inference or unresolved alternative.
- **4:** The obstacle explains the goal gap, evidence is relevant, assumptions are explicit, and alternatives have been tested.

## Overall Thinking Score anchors

- **0:** No evaluable problem frame.
- **1:** The frame is predominantly solution-led, ambiguous, or incoherent.
- **2:** The frame is directionally useful but incomplete.
- **3:** The frame is valid enough to support the next thinking step.
- **4:** The frame is clear, bounded, evidence-backed, and decision-ready.

## Calibration rule

Evaluators must cite observations supporting each score. When evidence fits two levels, choose the lower level unless the higher level's defining condition is demonstrated.
